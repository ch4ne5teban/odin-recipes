Smalahove - Norwegian delitrocity

Description

Smalahove is a traditional Norwegian dish made of sheep's head. Yup, the entire head with eyes and sometimes even the brain. It's typically had on the Sunday before Christmas with mashed potatoes and sausage.

Ingredients

  • Water 5 liters
  • Salt 1 kg
  • Sugar 125 g
  • Rutabagas 1 kg
  • Potatoes 4 medium-sized
  • Butter 100 g
  • Sugar 1 teaspoon
  • Nutmeg 2 teaspoons
  • White pepper 2 teaspoons
  • Mutton stock 250 ml

Steps:

  1. Take a large, sharp knife and cut the sheep's head in half lengthwise.
  2. Fill a large cooking pot with cold water and place the head in pot, leave for 24 hours.
  3. After 24 hours combine 4L of water with 1kg of salt, 1 cup of sugar and 3 tsp of saltpetre in order to make a brine.
  4. Dry the sheep’s head well and then place in to the brine mixture to sit for a further 24 hours.
  5. Leave to dry on a rack before placing it in a fresh pan of boiling water. Cover and cook for 1 hour.
  6. Whilst the sheep's head is cooking, peel and chop the potatoes and rutabaga and boil together in a pot of water for 30 minutes.
  7. Drain the potatoes and rutabaga, nutmeg, white pepper, mutton stock, butter and cream, and mash to a creamy consistency. Salt to taste.
  8. Take the sheep’s head out of the pan and serve immediately with the mashed potatoes and rutabaga.
